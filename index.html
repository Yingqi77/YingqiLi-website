Film and television production

The nature of the film determines the size and type of production team. Many Hollywood adventure films require computer-generated images (English: CGI) produced using CG painting techniques, so a large number of 3D model designers, animators, motion painters and other personnel are needed. However, an independent film with a lower budget may be produced by a small team of workers, often with a lower salary. Every part of the world uses a variety of techniques, shows, and genre styles for film production, and also has very different budget ranges, from government-funded sponsorships to studio systems for commercial film production.

Post-production (often abbreviated as "post-production") is the process of putting together all the elements of a finished film. Modern filmmaking is divided into two different workflows: the full use of traditional film negative (film), or a mix of traditional film negative and digital film (film and video). In the traditional film negative workflow, the original camera negative is first developed and copied into a single-light workprint for editing on a mechanical editing device.
